About

I’m Doug Paul, a retired software engineer. I live in upstate New York, in the Burned-over District1. The phrase “burned-over district”2 has had a negative spiritual connotation for some at least since Charles Finney used the phrase to describe “western New York”3. I call this blog “My Burned-over District” because I want to take a personal look at the spiritual issues here in the area and also I certainly want to take any due blame for perpetuating anything harmful arising from those spiritual issues.

I want to focus on the current spiritual situation of the area by means of historical and theological analysis. I think it should be possible to find connections between the past and the present. I am interested in the theological sciences as well as several other subjects. In this blog I hope to write about the theological sciences, especially Historical Theology, Systematic Theology, Hermeneutics, and related disciplines. I might also occasionally write about Computer Science or my favorite tree family (Fagaceae, the Beech family of trees).

I hope to post articles, book reviews and notices, and perhaps an essay now and then about the following subjects and/or others.

  • Apologetics
  • Assurance of Salvation
  • The Banner Ethos
  • Baptism, Communion, and the Great Tradition
  • Biographies
  • Canon
  • Confessions
  • Dispensationalism and Convenantalism
  • History, Philosophy, and Methods of the Theological Sciences
  • Key Books
  • Law & Gospel
  • LCF Baptists
  • Primary, Secondary, and Tertiary Doctrine
  • The Reformation
  • Revival, Evangelism, Discipleship, Church Growth, Revivalism
  • Speculative Theology

  1. Cross, Whitney R. The Burned-Over District: The Social and Intellectual History of Enthusiastic Religion in Western New York, 1800-1850. Ithaca and London: Cornell University Press, 1981. The book provides an influential secular analysis of Second Great Awakening and its subsequent impact in upstate New York. ↩

  2. Finney himself uses the phrase “burnt district” in his memoir. ↩

  3. By “western New York” Cross means all of New York State “west of the Catskills and Adirondack Mountains” [The Burned-Over District, page 4]. ↩
